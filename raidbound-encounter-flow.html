Raidbound Systems

Encounter Flow

This page focuses on how Raidbound combat reads from the player side: how a fight starts, where attention is directed, how feedback lands, and why encounters are structured to feel readable instead of chaotic.

Back to Home

Overview

Raidbound is built around tactical encounter readability. The goal is not just to show numbers and abilities, but to make it clear when a fight shifts, when pressure rises, and what the player should care about next.

Pre-Combat Framing

Combat splash screens, enemy count callouts, and encounter type labeling help establish context before the first action is taken.

Action Clarity

Hits, healing, floating numbers, shakes, and timing windows all support a combat flow that is easy to follow moment to moment.

Threat & Pressure

Threat generation and role responsibilities help the party feel coordinated rather than like isolated units taking turns.

Post-Fight Rhythm

Victory overlays, reward pacing, and transitions back to map or progression screens are treated as part of the full combat loop.

Why it matters

  • Supports a portfolio story centered on systems ownership and combat presentation.
  • Shows that the project is being shaped through deliberate UX and pacing choices.
  • Helps separate Raidbound from “prototype footage only” by documenting design intent.
